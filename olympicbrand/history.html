Olympic History Highlights

  • 1896: First Modern Olympics held in Athens, Greece.
  • 1900: Women participate in the Olympics for the first time in Paris.
  • 1936: Jesse Owens wins 4 gold medals in Berlin, challenging Nazi ideology.
  • 1960: Wilma Rudolph wins 3 golds in Rome, becoming "the fastest woman in the world".
  • 1964: Tokyo hosts the first Olympics in Asia, showcasing new technology.
  • 1980: US leads boycott of Moscow Olympics due to political tensions.
  • 1984: Carl Lewis wins 4 gold medals in Los Angeles.
  • 1992: Barcelona Olympics celebrates global unity after Cold War.
  • 2000: Cathy Freeman lights the cauldron in Sydney, inspiring Australia.
  • 2008: Michael Phelps wins 8 gold medals in Beijing, breaking records.
